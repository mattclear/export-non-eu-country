Part of Export to a non-EU country: step by step

  1. Step 1 Get your business ready to export

    1. Find overseas customers
    2. Research the countries you want to export to

    You need an EORI number to trade goods with countries outside the EU.

    1. Get an EORI number

  2. Step 2 Find a courier

    You can use a courier to help:

    • get your goods to the buyer
    • complete customs paperwork
    1. Find a courier

  3. Step 3 Check if you need to apply for a licence

    You may need to follow special rules or get a licence to export certain goods.

    1. Check if you need a licence for the goods you want to export
    2. You are currently viewing: Check the rules for the country you're exporting to

  4. Step 4 Prepare your goods for transport

    1. Insure your goods

    You must complete an invoice. You'll need to include the value of your goods and the correct commodity code. Your courier may be able to help you with this.

    1. Find the right commodity code for your goods
    2. Value your goods

    Attach the completed invoice and any licences you need to your shipment.

  5. Step 5 Keep invoices and records

    You must keep records of commercial invoices and any customs paperwork for 6 years.
